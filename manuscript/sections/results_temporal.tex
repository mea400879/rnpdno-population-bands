\subsection{Temporal Dynamics (RQ3)}
\label{sec:temporal}

Having established that the four statuses cluster in different places, we now examine how those spatial patterns have evolved since 2015.
Table~\ref{tab:slopes} reports OLS trend slopes (Newey-West HAC standard errors, bandwidth = 12) for HH municipality counts by region and status.

\input{tables/table6_trend_slopes.tex}

\subsubsection{The Centro Expansion}

Centro emerges as the fastest-growing hotspot region across all four statuses.
Not Located HH municipalities in Centro grew at $+4.96$ municipalities per year ($p < 0.001$), the largest slope in the entire region--status matrix.
Total ($+3.43$, $p < 0.001$), Located Alive ($+3.63$, $p < 0.001$), and Located Dead ($+1.26$, $p < 0.001$) all show significant positive trends.

Figure~\ref{fig:hh_trend} illustrates the divergent trajectories of Centro, Norte, and Baj\'{i}o.
The Centro expansion is visible as a steep upward trajectory beginning around 2019 for Not Located and accelerating through 2025.
This pattern was not documented in prior annual analyses focused on the Norte--Baj\'{i}o axis \citep{cadena_garrocho_2019}.
The mechanisms underlying this expansion are examined in Section~\ref{sec:disc_regional}.

\subsubsection{The Norte Consolidation}

Norte shows consistent positive growth in HH municipalities across all four statuses: Total $+0.62$ ($p = 0.014$), Not Located $+0.59$ ($p = 0.002$), Located Alive $+1.06$ ($p < 0.001$), and Located Dead $+0.78$ ($p < 0.001$).
The simultaneous growth across all four statuses, including both Located Alive and Located Dead, distinguishes Norte from all other regions.
Interpretation of this multi-status consolidation is deferred to Section~\ref{sec:disc_regional}.

\subsubsection{Baj\'{i}o Industrial Corridor: A Structural Break at 2017}

Earlier annual analyses \citep{cadena_garrocho_2019} suggested a rising Baj\'{i}o pattern driven by cartel territorial expansion into the Guanajuato--Jalisco corridor.
Our initial examination using the broad CESOP regional classification ($\sim$200 municipalities) found a negative Total slope, but this aggregate masked the dynamics of the narrower industrial corridor.
When the analysis is restricted to the 29-municipality Baj\'{i}o corridor defined in Section~\ref{sec:trend}, a Chow test identifies a structural break at February 2017 ($F = 78.13$, $p < 0.001$).
Prior to this break, HH municipalities within the corridor were declining ($\beta_1 = -8.82$ municipalities/year).
After 2017, the corridor shows a slow but statistically significant expansion ($\beta_2 = +0.47$/year, $p = 0.046$), driven primarily by Not Located cases ($+0.50$/year, $p < 0.001$).
Located Dead shows a positive but modest trend ($+0.15$/year, $p = 0.046$), while Located Alive declines ($-0.35$/year, $p = 0.003$).
The Total corridor slope is $-0.31$/year ($p = 0.041$), reflecting the net balance of a growing Not Located footprint against a shrinking Located Alive footprint.

The ``rising Baj\'{i}o'' hypothesis thus finds qualified support in the post-2017 period. 
The corridor is gaining Not Located HH municipalities, that is, places where people 
disappear and are not found, even as the overall number of hotspots remains broadly stable.
This temporal pattern is consistent with the intensification of cartel competition in 
Guanajuato's industrial municipalities, particularly around fuel infrastructure and 
territorial control \citep{reyes_robo_2022}. It also aligns with security analyses from 
the same period and complements the evidence reported by \citet{cadena_garrocho_2019}, 
whose dataset ended in 2017 during the ascending phase that our extended series confirms.

\subsubsection{Bookend Comparison}

Figure~\ref{fig:lisa_comparison} compares LISA maps for \bookendstart{} and \referencemonth{} for Located Alive and Located Dead.
Located Alive HH municipalities nearly doubled, from 60 in \bookendstart{} to 110 in \referencemonth{}, expanding from a concentrated Norte--Centro nucleus to a broader multi-regional pattern.
Located Dead clusters grew from 6 to 35 HH municipalities but remain sparse relative to the other statuses; a new cluster in Guanajuato emerges by 2025.

Figure~\ref{fig:heatmap} provides a synoptic view of regional dynamics through a heatmap of HH municipality counts across 11 June snapshots (2015--2025) for all four statuses.
Chi-square tests for distributional change between \bookendstart{} and \referencemonth{} are significant for three of four statuses: Not Located ($\chi^2 = 16.5$, $df = 4$, $p = 0.002$), Located Alive ($\chi^2 = 17.7$, $df = 4$, $p = 0.001$), and Located Dead ($\chi^2 = 14.1$, $df = 4$, $p = 0.007$; some expected cell frequencies fall below 5).
Total reaches the conventional threshold ($\chi^2 = 9.5$, $df = 4$, $p = 0.050$).
