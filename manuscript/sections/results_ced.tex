\subsection{CED Alignment}
\label{sec:ced}

Figure~\ref{fig:ced_states} presents HH dynamics for the eight states named in the CED Article~34 decision \citep{CED_MEX_art34_2026}.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig9_ced_states.pdf}
  \caption{Monthly count of HH municipalities in eight CED-named states by outcome status, \studyperiod{}.
           Shaded bands indicate CED-referenced temporal windows.}
  \label{fig:ced_states}
\end{figure}

Estado de M\'{e}xico shows the strongest growth: Not Located HH municipalities increased at $+2.36$ per year ($p < 0.001$) and Located Alive at $+1.81$ per year ($p < 0.001$), fully aligned with the CED's temporal window (2015 to 2025).
Nuevo Le\'{o}n exhibits multi-status growth, with Located Alive at $+1.34$/yr ($p < 0.001$) and Located Dead at $+0.80$/yr ($p < 0.001$), with HH peaks in 2022 to 2023.

Guanajuato presents a compositional shift: Not Located HH municipalities are rising ($+0.75$/yr, $p < 0.001$) while Located Alive is declining ($-0.57$/yr, $p < 0.001$).
The CED's finding of an eightfold increase since 2017 aligns with the Not Located trajectory but not with the Located Alive decline.

Jalisco registrations declined sharply after 2021, from 3,825 total persons (2021) to 216 (2024), with a corresponding decline in municipalities reporting cases (103 to 41) and no significant HH trend in any status. Coahuila, Veracruz, and Nayarit show low or zero HH municipalities throughout most of the study period.
The CED's temporal claims for these states reference crisis periods (2009 to 2017) that largely predate our study window.

Tabasco is flagged by the CED for an exponential increase in 2024 to 2025 with specific concern for girls and women.
Female Located Alive produced 31 cumulative municipality-month HH classifications across 6 municipalities over 2023 to 2025, exceeding the male equivalent (6 municipality-months across 4 municipalities).
For Not Located, the pattern is reversed (male 39 municipality-months vs.\ female 34), and overall HH counts in Tabasco remain low.
