\subsection{Descriptive Overview}
\label{sec:descriptive}

Figure~\ref{fig:timeseries} presents the national monthly time series for each outcome status, disaggregated by sex.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig1_time_series_by_status.pdf}
  \caption{Monthly registered persons by outcome status, Mexico, \studyperiod{}.
           Lines show total (solid), male (dashed), and female (dotted)
           case counts aggregated nationally.}
  \label{fig:timeseries}
\end{figure}

A prominent feature of the time series is the sustained acceleration of new registrations across the study period.
New Not Located registrations grew from approximately 784 per month in January 2015 to over 1,100 per month by mid-2025---a sustained upward trajectory reflecting a worsening crisis rather than a statistical artefact.
Located Dead registrations, though smaller in absolute magnitude, show a similar pattern: monthly counts roughly doubled from approximately 130 in early 2015 to over 180 by early 2025.
Located Alive registrations dominate the Total series numerically, contributing approximately 60\% of all monthly registrations, and exhibit the sharpest acceleration in the early study years.

The sex composition varies markedly across statuses.
Not Located is 77.9\% male, consistent with the established male-dominated profile of disappearance victimization in contexts of organized criminal violence.
Located Dead is even more skewed at 85.9\% male, closely aligned with homicide patterns.
Located Alive, however, approaches near-parity: 49.4\% male and 50.5\% female.
This compositional divergence---men dominate the population that remains missing, while recoveries are gender-balanced---is revisited in Section~\ref{sec:sex}.

Table~\ref{tab:summary} reports distributional statistics for monthly case counts across municipalities.
All four statuses exhibit extreme right-skew: the vast majority of municipality-months record zero cases, while a small number of municipalities contribute disproportionately large counts.
For Not Located, 85.7\% of municipality-months in \referencemonth{} record zero cases; for Located Dead, this figure reaches 96.3\%.
