\begin{abstract}                                                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                                            
  More than 132{,}000 persons are officially registered as disappeared and not located in Mexico, yet the government registry that documents
  this crisis, the National Registry of Disappeared and Unlocated                                                                                                                             
  Persons (Registro Nacional de Personas Desaparecidas y No                                                                                                                                 
  Localizadas, RNPDNO), provides no public API, no bulk-download                                                                                                                              
  facility, and no machine-readable data export.
  The portal employs session-based authentication, CAPTCHA                                                                                                                                    
  verification, and rate limiting; individual queries return                                                                                                                                
  dynamically rendered HTML tables for single                                                                                                                                                 
  municipality-status-date combinations.
  We constructed an original extraction pipeline                                                                                                                                              
  (Python, \texttt{requests}, \texttt{BeautifulSoup4}) that                                                                                                                                 
  systematically queried the portal across all combinations of
  2{,}478~municipalities, four victim-status categories, and monthly
  date ranges from January~2015 through December~2025.                                                                                                                                        
  Raw records were processed through a deterministic cleaning pipeline
  with two-pass geographic normalization against the INEGI municipal                                                                                                                          
  geo-code catalog.                                                                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                                              
  The dataset is organized into four CSV files by victim-status
  category:                                                                                                                                                                                   
  (i)~all registered persons (\texttt{status\_id}~=~0; 57{,}374 rows);                                                                                                                      
  (ii)~disappeared and not located (\texttt{status\_id}~=~7; 34{,}618 rows);
  (iii)~located alive (\texttt{status\_id}~=~2; 37{,}379 rows);                                                                                                                               
  (iv)~located deceased (\texttt{status\_id}~=~3; 9{,}936 rows).
  Each row represents a unique municipality--year--month cell with                                                                                                                            
  sex-disaggregated counts.                                                                                                                                                                 
  Standardized five-digit INEGI geo-codes enable direct spatial joins                                                                                                                         
  to census and cartographic data.                                                                                                                                                          
  Four sentinel geo-codes flag 4{,}163 rows (12{,}232 persons) with                                                                                                                           
  unresolvable geographic information.
  The complete pipeline and all manual override files are released in                                                                                                                         
  an open-source repository.                                                                                                                                                                
  \end{abstract}

\section*{Value of the Data}
\begin{itemize}

  \item Mexico's disappearance crisis is among the most severe
    human rights emergencies in the Western Hemisphere:
    more than 132{,}000 persons are officially registered as
    disappeared and not located as of early 2026, a figure that
    has nearly quadrupled since 2015
    \cite{mandolessi_2022, cnb_rnpdno}.
    In March 2026, the UN Committee on Enforced Disappearances
    concluded that enforced disappearances in Mexico constitute
    crimes against humanity \cite{CED_MEX_art34_2026}.
    The 2017 \emph{Ley General en Materia de Desaparici\'{o}n
Forzada} (General Act on Enforced Disappearance of Persons,
Disappearance Committed by Private Individuals, and the
National Search System) mandated a national registry and
search infrastructure \cite{ley_general_2017}, but the resulting
    RNPDNO portal provides no API, no bulk download, and no
    machine-readable export; access requires iterative queries
    through a CAPTCHA-protected interface that returns dynamically
    rendered HTML for single municipality, status, and date
    combinations.
    This dataset is the first machine-readable, analysis-ready
    extraction of the complete registry at national scale
    (2{,}475~municipalities, 132~monthly periods, 2015 to 2025),
    removing a critical barrier to independent computational
    research on disappearances in Mexico.

  \item The dataset serves researchers across multiple
    disciplines: criminologists studying the spatial diffusion
    of cartel-related violence \cite{atuesta_2024};
    human rights scholars documenting enforced disappearance
    patterns \cite{mandolessi_2022};
    public health researchers analyzing violence as an
    epidemiological phenomenon \cite{das_2025};
    and policy analysts evaluating search-commission resource
    allocation.
    The four-file structure by victim-status category
    (total, disappeared and not located, located alive,
    located deceased) enables outcome-transition analyses
    (location rates, resolution-time proxies, and spatial
    heterogeneity in case outcomes) that existing single-status
    or single-city studies cannot support
    \cite{aguilar_velazquez_2025}.

  \item Monthly temporal resolution across 132~periods
    (January~2015 through December~2025) supports sub-annual
    analyses (event-study designs around policy interventions,
    seasonal decomposition, and lagged-response modeling) that
    are not possible with the annual or cumulative snapshots
    used in prior municipality-level studies
    \cite{cadena_garrocho_2019, atuesta_2024}.

  \item The original RNPDNO portal reports geographic information
    only as free-text municipality names and numeric state
    identifiers, with no standardized geo-codes.
    Our pipeline assigns each record a five-digit INEGI geo-code
    (\texttt{cvegeo}) through two-pass normalization against the
    official municipal catalog, an original contribution that
    enables direct spatial joins to cartographic boundaries
    (EPSG:6372), population projections (CONAPO), and census
    microdata without intermediate reconciliation
    \cite{inegi_ageeml}.
    This standardization is a prerequisite for
    population-normalized rate computation and spatial cluster
    detection (LISA, Getis-Ord~$G_i^*$) \cite{anselin_1995}.
    Sex-disaggregated counts (male, female, undefined) at the
    municipality and month level further permit gendered analyses
    of disappearance patterns, a dimension largely absent from
    existing national-scale data products.
    
  \item The open-source processing pipeline, including scraper,
    text normalizer, two-pass geographic join, and git-tracked
    manual override files, can be re-executed against future
    RNPDNO releases to extend temporal coverage;
    schema-validation modules detect source drift, supporting
    longitudinal research programs \cite{reyes_where_2025}.
    This extraction-and-standardization approach parallels
    established methodologies for constructing research-grade
    datasets from computationally inaccessible government
    portals \cite{fazekas_2024}.

\end{itemize}

\section*{Experimental Design, Materials, and Methods}

\subsection*{Data source and access}

Data were obtained from the publicly accessible sociodemographic
query interface of the RNPDNO, maintained by the Comisión Nacional
de Búsqueda (CNB), available at:
\url{https://versionpublicarnpdno.segob.gob.mx/SocioDemografico/TablaDetalle}.
The interface presents HTML tables aggregated by Mexican state
(INEGI codes 01--32), year (2015--2025), and calendar month (1--12),
for four victim-status categories
(\texttt{id\_estatus\_victima}: 0, 2, 3, 7).
No bulk-download functionality is provided; access requires
iterative HTTP requests with a valid session cookie.

\subsection*{Web scraping}

The script \texttt{scripts/scrapers/scrape\_rnpdno\_single\_status.py} (Python~3.10)
implements a single-status scraper that iterates over all
combinations of state $\times$ year $\times$ month for one
status value, using the \texttt{requests} library for HTTP
communication and \texttt{BeautifulSoup4} for parsing HTML tables
embedded in JSON API responses.
Requests are serialized with a 0.5~s inter-state and 1.0~s
inter-month delay to avoid server overload; a retry mechanism
with exponential backoff (up to four retries, 45~s timeout)
handles transient failures. Four independent invocations produce one Parquet file per status value, consolidated from individual CSV exports per
  year--month pair (each covering all 33 states).  
State~33 (\emph{Se desconoce}, ``unknown'') is included in the iteration
to capture records with unresolvable state-level geography,
which are subsequently assigned sentinel geo-code
\texttt{cvegeo}~=~\texttt{99998}.

\subsection*{Geographic reference construction}

\subsection*{Geographic reference construction}

The INEGI \emph{Catálogo de Claves de Áreas Geoestadísticas
Estatales, Municipales y Localidades} (AGEEML), obtained from
\cite{inegi_ageeml}, provides the canonical municipality-level
geographic reference.
The function \texttt{build\_reference()} in
\texttt{scripts/\allowbreak{}processing/\allowbreak{}clean\_pipeline.py}
reads the raw AGEEML file (ISO-8859-1 encoding, 2{,}478 rows),
zero-pads geo-codes to canonical widths
(\texttt{CVEGEO}: 5~digits; \texttt{CVE\_ENT}: 2~digits;
\texttt{CVE\_MUN}: 3~digits), and derives two normalized
name variants using utilities in
\texttt{scripts/\allowbreak{}processing/\allowbreak{}normalize.py}:         
  2 digits; \texttt{CVE\_MUN}: 3 digits), and derives two normalized       
  name variants using utilities in                                         
  \texttt{scripts/processing/normalize.py}:


\begin{description}
  \item[Standard normalization]
    Unicode accents are stripped via NFD decomposition,
    text is lower-cased, punctuation is removed, and
    whitespace is collapsed to single spaces.

  \item[Aggressive normalization]
    Applies standard normalization, then expands a curated
    abbreviation dictionary
    (e.g., \texttt{Dr.}$\to$\texttt{doctor},
    \texttt{Gral.}$\to$\texttt{general}),
    removes the filler word \emph{de}, and collapses
    repeated whitespace.
\end{description}  

\subsection*{Pre-join state corrections (B1 heuristic)}

  The function \texttt{load\_state\_corrections()} in 
  \texttt{scripts/processing/clean\_pipeline.py} reads a curated
  lookup table of municipality names that are unique nationwide in         
  AGEEML (i.e., appear in exactly one state).                              
  For raw RNPDNO records whose reported state does not match the
  unique AGEEML state for their municipality name, the state               
  identifier is corrected prior to the geographic join.                    
  This procedure applies 25 validated corrections stored in
  \texttt{data/manual/geo\_state\_corrections.csv}.   

\subsection*{Two-pass geographic join}

The main pipeline (\texttt{scripts/processing/clean\_pipeline.py}) matches each
raw record to an AGEEML entry via a two-pass procedure:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Pass 1 – standard join.}
    Inner join on $(\texttt{cve\_estado}, f_{\text{std}}(\texttt{municipio}))$.

  \item \textbf{Pass 2 – aggressive join (unambiguous only).}
    For records unmatched in Pass~1, inner join on
    $(\texttt{cve\_estado},$ $f_{\text{agg}}(\texttt{municipio}))$,
    restricted to keys that map to exactly one AGEEML entry
    (i.e., no ambiguity).
\end{enumerate}

Records unmatched after both passes proceed to manual
  overrides, sentinel assignment, and finally unresolved
  geo-code assignment (see below).   

\subsection*{Manual overrides}

A git-tracked file \texttt{data/manual/geo\_overrides.csv} (36 rows)
maps $(raw\_id\_estado, raw\_municipio) \to \texttt{cvegeo}$
for cases requiring human disambiguation
(e.g., politically renamed municipalities, cross-state
boundary ambiguities).
Overrides are applied after both join passes and before
sentinel assignment.

\subsection*{Sentinel geo-code assignment}

Records that remain unresolvable after overrides are assigned
sentinel geo-codes according to the logic in
\texttt{data/manual/special\_geo\_cases.csv}:

\begin{description}
  \item[\texttt{cvegeo = 99998}]
    Assigned to records with \texttt{id\_estado}~=~33
    (unknown state) in the source data.

  \item[\texttt{cve\_mun = 998}]
    Source municipality string indicates no municipal reference.

  \item[\texttt{cve\_mun = 999}]
    Source municipality string indicates municipality unknown.
\end{description}


\subsection*{Quality checks}

The pipeline executes the following automated quality checks
before writing output:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Schema validation: all twelve columns present with
    expected types.
  \item Duplicate detection: exact-row duplicates originating
    from source-data artefacts are identified and removed.
  \item Sex-total consistency:
    $\texttt{total} = \texttt{male} + \texttt{female}
    + \texttt{undefined}$ verified for every row.
  \item Temporal coverage: year range [2015,~2025] and month
    range [1,~12] verified.
  \item Cross-status consistency: the aggregate person-count
    in the status-0 (total) file is compared against the sum
    of the three sub-status files
    (\texttt{status\_id}~$\in\{2,3,7\}$).
    As of the reference scrape, the total file records
    269{,}507 persons while the sum of the three sub-status
    files yields 269{,}164 persons---the total file contains
    343 more persons than the sub-status sum ($0.127\%$),
    distributed across 55{,}558
    non-sentinel (municipality, year, month) cells.
    This near-exact agreement confirms internal consistency
    of the source system; the residual discrepancy is
    attributed to asynchronous updates in the RNPDNO portal.
\end{itemize}

Results are written to \texttt{logs/quality\_report.csv}.


\subsection*{Output assembly}                                                                                                                                                               
                                                            
  Records that remain unmatched after the two-pass join, manual
  overrides, and sentinel assignment are assigned to a dedicated
  \emph{unresolved} geo-code (\texttt{cvegeo}~=~\texttt{99999},
  \texttt{cve\_estado}~=~\texttt{99}, \texttt{state}~=~``unresolved'')                                                                                                                        
  rather than being discarded, preserving the full aggregate
  person-counts.                                                                                                                                                                              
  Across the four processed files, 4 rows (4 persons) carry 
  the unresolved code.                                                                                                                                                                        
                                                            
  Two municipality names account for all unresolved records.                                                                                                                                  
  \textbf{PUEBLO NUEVO} appears in the source under Durango
  (\texttt{id\_estado}~=~10), Guanajuato                                                                                                                                                      
  (\texttt{id\_estado}~=~11), and Sinaloa                   
  (\texttt{id\_estado}~=~25).
  AGEEML contains canonical entries for Durango                                                                                                                                               
  (\texttt{cvegeo}~=~\texttt{10023}) and Guanajuato
  (\texttt{cvegeo}~=~\texttt{11024}), both resolved through                                                                                                                                   
  the standard join; no municipality named Pueblo Nuevo exists                                                                                                                                
  in Sinaloa, suggesting a source-data error.
  \textbf{OCAMPO} appears under Ciudad de M\'{e}xico                                                                                                                                          
  (\texttt{id\_estado}~=~9); AGEEML lists Ocampo in six                                                                                                                                       
  other states (Coahuila, Chihuahua, Durango, Guanajuato,                                                                                                                                     
  Michoac\'{a}n, Tamaulipas) but none in Ciudad de M\'{e}xico,                                                                                                                                
  and the correct target state cannot be determined.                                                                                                                                          
  Because neither case can be disambiguated, they are assigned                                                                                                                                
  \texttt{cvegeo}~=~\texttt{99999}.                                                                                                                                                           
                                                            
  No rows with null \texttt{cvegeo} values remain in the                                                                                                                                      
  processed files.                                          
  The four processed CSV files are written to
  \texttt{data/processed/}.

\section*{Ethics Statement}

The RNPDNO dataset is derived exclusively from publicly accessible
administrative records published by the Mexican federal government
through the Comisión Nacional de Búsqueda.
All data are presented in aggregated, municipality-level form;
no individual-level identifiers (names, dates of birth, case
numbers, or biometric data) are present in any of the processed
files.
Accordingly, no Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval or
ethics committee clearance is required for secondary analysis
of these public administrative data.
The collection and processing activities described here are
consistent with applicable data-use conditions stated by the CNB
for publicly disseminated RNPDNO statistics.
